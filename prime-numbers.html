Least common multiple of two primes is their product

Let \(p_1, p_2\) be prime. We want the smallest integer \(q\) such that both \(p_1\) and \(p_2\) divide \(q\).

Suppose \(p_1 > p_2\) and let \(q = ap_1\). \(a\) can not be 1 since \(p_1\) is prime and therefore \(p_2\) does not divide it. In general, the factors of \(ap_1\) are \(p_1\), and integers equal to or smaller than \(a\). Therefore the first value of \(a\) for which \(p_2\) divides \(ap_1\) is \(p_2\). Therefore the least common multiple of \(p_1\) and \(p_2\) is their product \(p_2p_1\).
